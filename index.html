EAGLE
Eagle is the common name for the golden eagle, bald eagle, and other birds of prey in the family of the Accipitridae. Eagles belong to several groups of genera, some of which are closely related. True eagles comprise the genus Aquila. Most of the 68 species of eagles are from Eurasia and Africa.

CROW
Crows are highly intelligent birds known for their problem-solving skills and social behavior. They belong to the Corvidae family and are found in many habitats worldwide. Recognizable by their glossy black feathers and distinctive cawing calls, crows are also known for their ability to use tools and communicate with each other effectively.

BUTTERFLY
Butterflies are colorful insects belonging to the order Lepidoptera, known for their stunning wing patterns and delicate structure. They undergo a remarkable metamorphosis, transforming from caterpillars to winged adults. As important pollinators, butterflies play a vital role in ecosystems and are often seen as symbols of transformation and beauty.

OWL
Owls are nocturnal birds of prey known for their distinctive round faces, large eyes, and silent flight. They have excellent night vision and can rotate their heads up to 270 degrees. With over 200 species, owls play crucial roles in controlling rodent populations and are often associated with wisdom in various cultures.

KINGFISHER
Kingfishers are vibrant, agile birds known for their striking plumage and exceptional fishing skills. They typically inhabit freshwater habitats and use their keen eyesight to spot prey from above, diving swiftly to catch fish, crustaceans, and insects. With over 90 species worldwide, kingfishers are celebrated for their beauty and unique hunting techniques.

PARROT
Parrots are colorful, intelligent birds known for their strong beaks and ability to mimic sounds, including human speech. Found in tropical and subtropical regions, they have zygodactyl feet, allowing them to grasp branches with ease. With over 400 species, parrots are social creatures often seen in flocks, and they play vital roles in their ecosystems as seed dispersers.

HERON
Herons are wading birds characterized by their long legs, necks, and sharp beaks, which they use to catch fish and other aquatic prey. They are often found in wetlands, marshes, and along shorelines, standing still for long periods while hunting. With various species worldwide, herons are known for their graceful flight and striking silhouettes.
